The 6.92% Framework

How the average Tax Goddess client gets to a 6.92% effective tax rate. The four pillars, the order they get applied in, why most tax planning misses the order.

M2

Entity Structure For The Pre-$200K Operator

S-corp vs LLC vs sole prop math at the $50K-$200K revenue band. The thresholds where the entity choice actually moves the tax line. The mistakes that cost the next $50K of revenue in extra tax.

M3

Top 30 Deductions The Pre-$200K Operator Misses

The deductions that compound: home office, vehicle, retirement, family employment, R&D credit, accountable plan. With the actual line numbers, the documentation requirements, and the audit-defense framing.

M4

Retirement Stack As A Tax Lever

Solo 401(k), SEP-IRA, defined benefit plans for the high-income solo. How the contribution stack moves the tax line at the $100K-$300K income band, and the multi-year compounding that turns it into a wealth-building lever, not just a tax-avoidance lever.

M5

Real Estate As A Tax Strategy

Cost segregation, bonus depreciation, real estate professional status, short-term rental loopholes. How real-estate-as-tax-strategy works for the operator who isn't yet a full-time real-estate investor.

M6

R&D, Section 179, Bonus Depreciation Stack

The capex side of the tax stack. When to time equipment purchases, when to use the R&D credit, when bonus depreciation actually beats Section 179. With the worked examples for the pre-$200K operator.

M7

Audit Defense And Documentation

The documentation discipline that makes every above-the-line strategy defensible. The records, the contemporaneous logs, the bookkeeping infrastructure. Why most aggressive tax strategies fail at audit, and how Tax Goddess clients structure documentation to make audits a non-event.

M8

The Upgrade Path To STC

Where the self-paced framework caps and where Strategic Tax Coaching takes over. The revenue threshold where a multi-strategy plan needs human implementation. The Course-to-STC handoff for the alumni who've crossed the line.

Why would I run this with an outside operator instead of in-house?

Because the team you have is already running the firm. The 85 specialists are delivering. The consultation calendar is being managed. The cheat-sheet content is being produced. Outside operator is faster, cleaner, and exits when production hits steady state. We hand the system to your team after the launch cycle, not before.

How much of my time does this need?

Roughly half a day to record the homepage walkthrough video. One day to record the on-demand training. Will the Tax Strategy Course cannibalise STC?

No. The Course and STC solve different problems for different audiences. The Course is for the $50K-$200K revenue operator who's too small for STC right now and would otherwise bounce. STC is for the $200K-$1M operator who needs human implementation. Most Course buyers will not be STC candidates today; they'll be STC candidates two years from now once they cross the revenue threshold. The Course captures revenue from a segment currently lost, builds the relationship, and creates a structured upgrade path into STC when they qualify. Different deliverable, different audience, different buying motion. The overlap is feature, not bug.
